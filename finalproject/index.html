"There are cities that get by on their good looks, offer climate and scenery, view of mountains or oceans, rockbound or with palm trees. And then there are cities like Detroit that have to work for a living."

- Elmore Leonard

Detroit has over 300 years of history, along with some great places to get a drink. We here at the Detroit Drunken Historical Society like to connect people, whether they are native-born Detroiters or have adopted the city as their home, to the people and events that have shaped the "city upon the straits."

We are a nonhierarchical group of history nerds who began planning events in February 2012. We thought it would be fun to host lectures at local bars. Eventually we started to plan tours too. If you have an idea for an event and can find a speaker and a place to host, then we encourage you to plan it. Send us an email at detroitdrunkenhistorical@gmail.com or follow us on Twitter.

Cheers!

Lantern Lit Underground Railroad Tour

Saturday, September 20th, 2014 at 6pm at the Second Baptist Chruch of Detroit

There are several dates for this tour, but I am planning to go on Saturday evening, September 20th. It begins in Detroit at the Second Baptist Church at the corner of Monroe and Beaubien in Greektown. Karin Risko, the guide and organizer for Hometown History Tours will lead us along stops on Detroit's Underground Railroad, a very important part of our history. The tour is at least two and a half hours long, so wear comfortable shoes and dress appropriately! We will have dinner beforehand.
